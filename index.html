Why System73?

Broadcast Video over the Open Internet is the Future, providing Network Operators and Broadcasters with significant opportunities to Expand their Reach and Increase their ROI:

  • New Customers can be added from anywhere on the Internet via any connected device without requiring expensive proprietary infrastructure and without sacrificing compatibility with playout, analytics, support, and other existing systems.

  • Reliable Performance can be maintained by addressing Network Congestion and High Concurrent Usage in an otherwise uncontrolled Open Internet environment.

  • Content flexibility can be enhanced, enabling different classes of users to select among various content and pricing models.

  • Significant cost savings result in increased ROI and expanded customer offerings.


System73 exploits these opportunities in a manner that enables Network Operators and Broadcasters to Increase their ROI

System73 is a state-of-the-art worldwide video broadcasting system designed for use on the Open Internet. No expensive proprietary infrastructure is required - unlike cable and satellite systems, managed networks running on walled-off fiber rings, and VOD providers that rely on extensive edge-server infrastructure.

Yet, System73 is designed to be compatible with existing Broadcast Video infrastructure (including playout monitoring, support, analytics, and other administrative functionality), thereby enabling Network Operators and Broadcasters to expand their existing reach without investing in infrastructure. Expanding the user base beyond the constraints of proprietary infrastructure provides the flexibility to offer an assortment of content pricing models, which in turn translates into increased ROI.

Slow video startup times, jitter, buffering delays and other artifacts of existing Internet video streaming systems -- particularly during high-concurrent peak viewing times â€“ are a thing of the past. When viewers join a video channel, System73 immediately pushes the video stream to them, while monitoring viewing nodes and Internet peering points to predict congestion and proactively re-route video packages to ensure they reach each viewer in time.

System73 has developed the next generation of video transport protocols by continuing to refine and patent its underlying deep learning processes to predict Internet traffic congestion and its proprietary non-linear optimization techniques to generate optimal routes for video packages while leveraging the adoption of WebRTC, WebSocket, Adaptive Streaming and other Internet standards.

The result is a virtual broadcast network that enables Network Operators and Broadcasters to efficiently stream live high-definition video to an unlimited number of concurrent viewers over the Open Internet with the reliability of a fully managed network.
